ATTORNEYS

Sarah M. Neiley

Managing Partner

Sarah represents clients in sophisticated family matters involving the analysis and litigation of high asset marital estates, property and business valuations, complex incomes, challenging parenting issues, and prenuptial and postnuptial marital agreements. Though Sarah represents clients with sophisticated legal issues, she is equally passionate about her less complex cases. Family relationships and family systems are fundamental for individual wellness and success. For this reason, the legal issues that affect families are critically important.

Sarah understands the importance of her clients’ legal matters and how her work has long- lasting impacts in their lives and the lives of their families. She has an in-depth understanding of the legal issues her clients face and the experience to navigate them soundly by engaging in high-level analysis, strategizing, and problem-solving. Sarah guides her clients to make informed and carefully-considered decisions. In family law, resolution comes in many forms and looks different for every client. Sarah prides herself on listening to her clients and understanding their needs and goals to inform a strategy of client-oriented resolution. She encourages her clients to reach out-of-court settlements when possible and enjoys working with her clients and opposing parties to find creative resolutions.

Sarah believes it is critical to provide her clients with direct and honest advice about where to focus their time, energy, and money in litigating family law issues. Most of Sarah’s cases settle through negotiated agreements. However, when settlement is not possible, Sarah fervently advocates for her clients in court where she is a fierce opponent due to her extensive career as a trial attorney.  

During the early stages of her career, Sarah was a criminal prosecutor for the State of Colorado in both rural and metropolitan districts. She successfully managed and prosecuted hundreds, if not thousands, of criminal matters ranging from simple misdemeanors to felony offenses. After leaving the government, Sarah entered private practice and worked for one of the largest law firms on the Western Slope of Colorado as an associate in the firm’s family law and civil litigation departments. After starting a family of her own and recognizing her true passion for family law, Sarah founded and managed her own family law firm for five years before forming Neiley & Morris.

Sarah has personally tried over 60 cases, and she has taken even more cases to evidentiary hearings and hearings on legal argument. Due to her experience working in various jurisdictions and her unique background in family, civil, and criminal law, Sarah has the skillset and acumen to effectively manage, resolve, and litigate her client's legal issues. Sarah and the legal team at Neiley & Morris are dedicated to providing family law litigants with compassionate and efficient legal representation.

Mackenzie J. Morris

Managing Partner

Mackenzie is a dedicated Criminal Defense and Family Law Attorney who prioritizes client-centered representation, focusing on long-term outcomes that align with her clients' needs. With extensive experience in complex litigation, she has successfully represented clients in numerous trials and hearings throughout Colorado. While she values efficient resolutions and settlements when appropriate, her track record showcases her commitment to achieving favorable results in high-stakes cases.

Mackenzie’s passion for criminal defense was ignited early in her career when she joined a team of attorneys representing one of the three inmates on Colorado’s Death Row. This pivotal experience fueled her dedication to defending the constitutional rights of the accused and advocating against systemic abuses within the criminal justice system. She approaches each case with thorough legal analysis, empowering her clients to make informed decisions—whether negotiating a plea agreement or mounting a robust defense in front of a jury.

Before entering private practice, Mackenzie served as a Deputy Public Defender for the State of Colorado, where she represented hundreds of individuals charged wigh a wide range of crimes, from attempted homicide and sex assaults, to DUIs and minor drug offenses. This background has sharpened her litigation and negotiation skills, making her a compassionate and effective advocate in family law as well. Most of Mackenzie’s family law cases result in efficient settlements, as she strives to find creative solutions that lead to equitable outcomes and minimize conflict for families.

Mackenzie is consistently praised by her clients for her attention to detail and her ability to provide comfort and support while her clients navigate the emotional complexities of family law. Mackenzie earned her law degree from the University of Denver Sturm College of Law, where she was honored as the Outstanding Student Attorney in Criminal Defense from the nationally ranked criminal defense clinic. During her time in law school, she also received the Public Good Distinction Award, the Reece Persuasive Writing Award, was the recipient of two scholarships for trial advocacy, and served as President of the Public Interest Law Group. Prior to her legal studies, she earned a B.A. in Political Science and International Studies from the University of Colorado and worked as a legislative representative for a state-wide nonprofit.

Outside of her legal practice, Mackenzie is an avid outdoor enthusiast. She enjoys mountain biking, fishing, snowboarding, rafting, and is a dedicated hiker who has summited many of Colorado’s 14ers. Mackenzie loves to travel and has hiked across the world, including a 130- mile trek to Everest Base Camp.

PARALEGALS

Zoe M. Dotson

Paralegal

Zoe is one of the firm’s paralegals. Though Zoe works in all areas of practice, her main practice area is Family Law. She is a critical member of our Legal Team who has a keen eye and natural ability to identify and analyze important facts, evidence, legal issues. Zoe works with efficiency and is dedicated to ensuring that our team and clients are prepared and equipped to achieve resolution, whether through a negotiated settlement or court hearing. Zoe provides great care and understanding to our clients who enjoy working with her and know that she is invested in their individual cases and successes. Zoe began working as a paralegal with our attorneys in early 2022. Prior to working as a paralegal, Zoe held the positions of both Account Clerk and Court Clerk for the Garfield County Combined Courts. During her time as a clerk, Zoe gained intimate familiarity with the court’s documentation and filing processes, and she was responsible for managing and balancing the court’s registry account for the entire Ninth Judicial District. Zoe is a native of the Roaring Fork Valley, and in combination with her background as a court clerk, her knowledge and resourcefulness are great assets to our firm and clients.
